S=Vit+1/2at2:The Equation of Linear Equation

The formula S=Vit+1/2at2:The Equation of Linear Equation describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration.
Here,

  • S represents the displacement
  • Vi is the initial velocity
  • t is the time
  • a is the acceleration
  • 1/2at2 is the additional displacement due to acceleration.

An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Note:This equation was written in 1905.
